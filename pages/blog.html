Things I Have Learned. Week 2, Day 5.

Now that the code is getting bigger and more complicated, it’s useful to keep an eye on what your code is producing.

Something as simple as an HDMI cable can easily transform your coding experience, especially if you have a small screen laptop.

Method 1: HDMI Cable

Check out this product on Amazon (Affiliate Link) (other brands are available!).

Plug one end into the laptop, plug the other end into the nearest TV, and set up dual screen. Use your laptop screen to input the code and watch the TV to see real-time changes!

You’re welcome.

Method 2: Wireless Display Adapters

If you prefer a wireless setup, consider using devices like Google Chromecast or the Microsoft Wireless Display Adapter.

These allow you to stream your computer’s display wirelessly to your TV. Just follow the device-specific instructions to connect your computer to your TV.

Method 3: Smart TV Apps

Some smart TVs have built-in apps or support screen mirroring technologies like Miracast, AirPlay, or Chromecast. You can use these features to mirror your computer screen to your TV wirelessly.

Method 4: DisplayPort or USB-C to HDMI Adapter

If your computer has a DisplayPort or USB-C output, you can use an adapter to connect to the HDMI port on your TV. This is a great alternative if your laptop doesn’t have an HDMI port.

Steps to Set Up the TV as a Second Screen

  1. Connect the TV:
  2. Use the appropriate cable or wireless method to connect your TV to your computer.
  3. Configure Display Settings:
    • Windows:
      • Right-click on the desktop and select "Display settings."
      • You should see your TV listed as a second monitor. If not, click on "Detect."
      • Choose how you want to use the TV: "Duplicate" (mirror the display), "Extend" (use as a separate screen), or "Second screen only."
    • On macOS:
      • Go to "System Preferences" > "Displays."
      • You should see the TV listed as a second display. Adjust the settings to "Mirror Displays" or "Extend Desktop."
  4. Adjust Resolution and Scaling:
    • Ensure that the resolution and scaling settings are appropriate for your TV to get the best picture quality.

Using your TV as a second screen can be very beneficial for coding, as it allows you to have more screen real estate for your development environment and to easily view output or documentation.
